Project Overview: This project explores the design and implementation of automated cruise control and lane-changing strategies using a Mercedes AMG C63s model. The objective was to develop and validate control algorithms that enhance vehicle stability and performance under dynamic driving conditions.

Methodology:

  • Developed a P Controller for speed regulation.
  • Implemented a PD Controller for lane transitions to ensure smooth changes.
  • Simulated system performance under real-world conditions.

Findings:

  • P Controller effectively maintained speed but exhibited steady-state errors.
  • PD Controller improved lane change accuracy, reducing drift.
  • Future work involves adding an I component for enhanced precision.

Project Overview: This project focuses on the design of a state feedback controller to enhance vehicle ride stability and comfort. A quarter-car suspension model was developed to analyze suspension dynamics and control strategies.

Methodology:

  • Derived system equations based on Newton’s second law and modeled a quarter-car system.
  • Designed a state feedback controller with proportional gain tuning.
  • Validated the model using MATLAB and Simulink simulations.

Findings:

  • The designed controller effectively mitigated road disturbances, improving ride comfort.
  • Tested under step and sinusoidal road inputs, achieving expected stability and damping.
  • Future improvements include integrating a full-vehicle model and nonlinear dynamics for better accuracy.

Project Overview: This project evaluates the feasibility of hydrogen as a fuel in spark-ignition (SI) engines using three key approaches: thermodynamic modeling, heat transfer analysis, and emissions evaluation. A MATLAB-based zero-dimensional single-zone thermodynamic model was developed to simulate in-cylinder pressure, temperature dynamics, and combustion efficiency. Additionally, Annand’s heat transfer model was applied to analyze heat losses, and NOx emissions were assessed to evaluate environmental impact. The study provides a comprehensive analysis of hydrogen’s potential as a clean alternative to fossil fuels.

Methodology:

  • Developed a zero-dimensional single-zone thermodynamic model to simulate the in-cylinder combustion process of hydrogen-air mixtures.
  • Predicted cylinder pressure, temperature variations, and combustion efficiency under different operating conditions.
  • Applied Annand’s heat transfer model to evaluate heat losses through the cylinder walls.
  • Compared the impact of constant vs. variable wall temperature conditions on energy transfer efficiency.
  • Investigated NOx emissions formation during hydrogen combustion and its environmental implications.

Role: Research Contributor & MATLAB Developer

  • Developed a MATLAB-based zero-dimensional thermodynamic model to simulate combustion dynamics.
  • Implemented heat transfer analysis to assess energy losses under different operating conditions.
  • Facilitated research coordination, structured methodologies, and ensured effective communication within the team.
