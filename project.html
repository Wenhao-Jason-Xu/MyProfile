Showcasing my coursework-based engineering projects.

Project Overview: This project explores the design and implementation of automated cruise control and lane-changing strategies using a Mercedes AMG C63s model. The objective was to develop and validate control algorithms that enhance vehicle stability and performance under dynamic driving conditions.

Methodology:

  • Designed a Proportional (P) Controller to regulate vehicle speed while minimizing deviation.
  • Developed a Proportional-Derivative (PD) Controller for smooth and responsive lane-changing maneuvers.
  • Conducted extensive simulations to evaluate system performance under varying driving scenarios.

Key Findings:

  • The P Controller effectively maintained speed but exhibited steady-state errors in response to external disturbances.
  • The PD Controller enabled precise and stable lane changes, improving safety and maneuverability.
  • Future enhancements include integrating an Integral (I) Component to eliminate steady-state errors and further refine performance.
